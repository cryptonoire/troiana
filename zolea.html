Overview

Zolea is a community platform for the Solana crypto world. It blends the social structure of Discord — servers, channels, voice, DMs — with tools traders actually need, like live coin cards, on-chain analysis apps and one-click in-app buying. We designed and engineered the entire platform end to end: product design, full-stack architecture, real-time infrastructure, wallet integration and deployment.

The product is split across three connected surfaces under one brand: the main community app, a marketing site, and a suite of standalone Solana analysis tools.

One brand, three surfaces

Each domain serves a distinct job, while sharing one design language and account system.

What we built

Live coin cards

The product's differentiator — real-time, embeddable coin cards that bring live on-chain market data directly into the community feed.

Communities & channels

Discord-style servers with text and voice channels, roles, verified & official badges, and a discovery experience by category.

Real-time chat & presence

Live messaging, typing indicators, online/offline presence, last-seen and an invisible mode — all powered by Supabase Realtime.

End-to-end encrypted DMs

Private messages encrypted with ECDH + AES-GCM, keys held client-side, plus file attachments and paste-to-send screenshots.

Voice & private calls

LiveKit-powered voice channels and 1:1 E2E-encrypted voice + screen-share calls launched straight from a DM.

In-app Solana trading

One-click pump.fun buys with an on-chain 1% fee, signed by the user's Phantom wallet — non-custodial by design.

Solana analysis apps

A suite of standalone tools on apps.zolea.fun: rug checker, token security, holder & wallet analysis, bubble maps and developer insights.

Onboarding & admin

A guided first-run flow, an owner-only admin dashboard, friends & blocking, notifications, sponsor tiers and a desktop (Electron) shell.

Engineering highlights

Zolea is non-custodial by design — the platform has no ability to move user funds; every trade is signed locally by the user's own wallet. DM encryption keys never touch the server, and the call encryption key is derived from the same ECDH secret as the DM, so private calls inherit the same guarantees.

On the performance side, we moved presence to an external store, added a per-request DB-read cache at the proxy layer, and tuned real-time rendering to keep the app responsive as communities grow.
